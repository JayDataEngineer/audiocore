Voice Maker

Three mathematical knobs for shaping voices without re-encoding audio. Blend two voices with SLERP, push a voice along a steering vector, or discover new directions from labelled groups. Results are saved to your Voices library.

1

Blend two voices

linear · SLERP

Smoothly interpolate between voice A and voice B. At t=0 you hear pure A, at t=1 pure B, at t=0.5 an even blend. SLERP follows the great-circle arc on the unit hypersphere — smoother for cross-gender / cross-age blends.

2

Apply steering vector

linear algebra

Push a base voice along a labelled concept direction (e.g. gender_v2_f_minus_m.dir). Positive scale → more "positive" end of the concept; negative scale → opposite. Try scale = ±0.5 for subtle shifts, ±1.0 for stronger.

3

Discover new steering vector

mean(diff)

Build a brand-new concept direction from labelled examples. Pick which voices embody the "more X" end and which embody "less X." The vector emitted is mean(positive) − mean(negative). Reuse it in Knob 2 above.

4

Shape voice (pitch & speed)

WSOLA · sidecar

Fine-tune how a saved voice sounds without re-encoding audio. Adjust pitch (semitones) and speed — every future Synthesize call using this voice will apply the saved shaping automatically. Uses WSOLA time-domain pitch shifting so the speaker identity is preserved.
